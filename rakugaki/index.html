Rakugaki

Concept

My main inspiration for this project was combining traditional Japanese textiles and motifs with graffiti and street art - specifically Japanese inspired or motifs. I wanted to create a juxtaposition between the two and modernise traditional techniques that are becoming less and less engaged with. I also wanted to focus on the amelioration of street art, as it's seen as vandalism but when done well it has artistic value. Comparing and combining with beautiful traditional pieces hopefully shows the beauty of well crafted street art.

Brief

The project aim was a fabric collection of 20 fabrics for interior end use (upholstery, furnishings etc). Most designs had alternate colour ways and I stuck to a certain colour palette that I extracted from my initial research that I thought summed up my theme the most and wanted to work with. The collection was a mixture of digitally printed designs onto fabric and hand-stitched sashiko and boro samples. My motifs were created though hand-drawing, painting, stitch, collage and creating my own graffiti stencils using card and a craft knife. I have always loved the look of graffiti and street art so it was enjoyable to engage with graffiti techniques myself.
